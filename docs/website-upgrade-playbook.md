# Local Business Website Upgrade Playbook

## Objective
Create a repeatable system for identifying local businesses with weak websites and offering a website/conversion upgrade that helps them generate more leads.

## Initial Market
- City: San Jose
- Niche: Landscaping

## Why This Works
Many local service businesses have:
- outdated websites
- weak mobile experience
- poor contact/quote flows
- no clear trust signals
- weak branding
- low conversion from existing traffic

The pitch is not just "buy a website."
The pitch is:
**get more leads from the traffic and reputation you already have.**

---

## Offer Positioning
### Bad positioning
- I build websites
- I can redesign your site

### Better positioning
- I help local service businesses turn weak websites into lead-generating websites
- I improve mobile conversion, quote flow, and trust signals so more visitors become customers
- I build simple websites that help book more jobs

---

## Target Business Criteria
A strong prospect usually has:
- a real service business with visible demand
- an outdated or weak website
- poor mobile UX
- weak CTA or no CTA
- little/no quote form optimization
- few trust signals
- weak visual presentation
- active enough business presence that they can pay

---

## Prospect Scoring Framework
Score each category from 1 to 5.

### 1. Website Design Quality
- 1 = modern/professional
- 3 = okay but dated
- 5 = clearly weak/outdated

### 2. Mobile Experience
- 1 = clean mobile experience
- 3 = usable but clunky
- 5 = bad on mobile / broken / tiny text / hard to use

### 3. CTA Strength
- 1 = strong call/book/quote flow
- 3 = basic contact info only
- 5 = weak or unclear action path

### 4. Trust Signals
- 1 = reviews, testimonials, licenses, photos, before/after, badges
- 3 = some proof but thin
- 5 = little or no trust-building

### 5. Branding / Visual Quality
- 1 = polished brand
- 3 = average
- 5 = sloppy / generic / weak visuals

### 6. Lead Opportunity
- 1 = limited market upside
- 3 = reasonable local service demand
- 5 = strong service demand + obvious upside if conversion improves

### Suggested interpretation
- 22–30 = high-priority outreach
- 16–21 = medium-priority outreach
- 6–15 = low-priority outreach

---

## What To Look For On Their Website
- no clear “Get Quote” button
- no mobile click-to-call
- no before/after photos
- no trust section
- weak headline
- no service-area clarity
- no differentiated offer
- generic contact page only
- poor loading speed
- broken layout / dated design

---

## Outreach Strategy
### Goal
Start conversations by showing that you understand how their weak site is costing them leads.

### Outreach angle
- mention one or two specific problems
- connect that to lost leads / poor conversion
- keep it short
- offer a simple improvement, not a giant lecture

---

## Simple Offer Ladder
### Offer 1 — Starter
- 1–3 page website refresh
- mobile-first upgrade
- improved CTA + quote/contact path

### Offer 2 — Lead Gen Website
- full conversion-focused site
- service pages
- trust sections
- quote funnel
- call and form optimization

### Offer 3 — Ongoing Retainer
- updates
- landing pages
- local SEO improvements
- conversion tweaks

---

## Initial Sales Focus
Do not try to be everything.
Start by selling:
**website upgrade + better lead conversion**

That is much easier to explain and close.

---

## Recommended Workflow
1. build a prospect list
2. score each website
3. shortlist the best 20 prospects
4. send direct outreach
5. follow up
6. pitch a simple website/conversion upgrade
7. close 1–2 deals
8. document wins and repeat

---

## Expansion Path
Once the landscaping workflow works, extend into:
- demolition
- moving
- junk removal
- roofing
- HVAC
- plumbing
- any local service business with weak digital presence

---

## Core Principle
Do not sell aesthetics alone.
Sell:
- more leads
- easier contact
- more trust
- better conversion
